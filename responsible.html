Stay in control

Smart tools still need human wisdom.

AI can be useful, persuasive, and wrong at the same time. These habits help you benefit from it without trusting it blindly.

PrivacyFact-checkingHuman control
Six essential habits

Pause. Check. Decide.

Responsible use does not need to be complicated—it starts with healthy skepticism and clear boundaries.

Verify important facts

Check trustworthy sources before relying on claims that affect health, money, rights, safety, or reputation.

Protect privacy

Do not share passwords, identification numbers, private records, confidential files, or sensitive personal details.

Watch for bias

AI can reproduce unfair assumptions. Ask whose perspective may be missing and review outcomes carefully.

Keep human oversight

A person should remain responsible for meaningful decisions, especially when they affect other people.

Respect original work

Do not present copied or AI-generated material dishonestly. Credit sources and follow relevant rules.

Know when to stop

For high-stakes or deeply personal matters, seek a qualified professional rather than continuing to prompt.

A simple rule to remember

Use AI for assistance, not authority. Let it help you generate possibilities and organize thinking—but keep responsibility for the final choice.
