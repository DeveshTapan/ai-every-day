AI at work

Less busywork. More space for good judgment.

Use AI to accelerate first drafts, research, analysis, and routine workflows—then apply your expertise to make the result worthwhile.

Practical workflows

Move from blank page to useful work.

AI works best as a capable assistant—not an invisible decision-maker.

Write & communicate

Draft emails, improve tone, simplify explanations, and structure reports.

“Rewrite this update for a busy senior leader.”

Research a topic

Build an initial overview, identify questions, and organize findings for verification.

“Create a research checklist for this market topic.”

Analyze information

Summarize feedback, categorize themes, and suggest ways to inspect data.

“Group this customer feedback into recurring themes.”

Prepare meetings

Create agendas, discussion questions, notes, and clear follow-up actions.

“Turn these notes into decisions and action items.”

Design workflows

Document repeatable steps, templates, and opportunities for careful automation.

“Turn this process into a clear reusable checklist.”

Brainstorm options

Explore alternatives, risks, questions, and perspectives before deciding.

“Challenge this proposal from three viewpoints.”

Protect confidential information

Do not paste private customer data, internal documents, passwords, financial records, or proprietary information into an AI tool unless your organization has explicitly approved that use.
